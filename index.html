Lookalike: Style Matchmaker
Project Overview

The Lookalike: Style Matchmaker project aims to leverage unsupervised machine learning algorithms to find and categorize similar fashion styles for Inditex. Using image embeddings from advanced models like ResNet50, ViT, and SBERT, the project processes individual garments and outfits to better understand trends and offer recommendations.

Objectives:
  • Categorize garments into meaningful clusters based on their characteristics and style.
  • Find similar styles across different collections to enhance the user shopping experience.
  • Leverage advanced embeddings to understand and represent garment similarity.

Housing Market Analysis - Ames, Iowa

The project focuses on the characteristics of residential properties in Ames, Iowa, providing a deeper insight into the local real estate market and understanding how different housing features influence property values.

In general, this analysis aims to provide stakeholders in the real estate market with insights to make more informed decisions, promoting greater transparency in buying and selling properties in Ames.

Characterization of Global Tourism

Analyzing global tourism activities to understand trends, behaviors, and impacts on the tourism industry.

Characterization of Global Tourism

The project ‘Characterization of Global Tourism’ focuses on analyzing tourism activities in most countries worldwide and how they have evolved over the years. The primary aim is to explain behaviors, trends, and the consequences of various events that impact the tourism industry. This project involves a data-driven exploration of how tourism has changed, influenced by key events, and what we can learn from the data to anticipate future changes.

Objectives:
  • Country Grouping: Cluster countries based on tourism trends they experienced during different time intervals (e.g., beginning of a crisis, pre-2000s, etc.).
  • Inbound vs. Outbound Travel: Analyze the relationship between the inflow and outflow of people in each country to establish a "tourism index" and examine events that significantly influence this ratio.
  • Transportation Evolution: Study the evolution of means of transportation used to access different countries, compare popular transportation methods across different periods, and identify shifts in trends.
  • Tourist Spending & Stay: Analyze the correlation between the number of tourists arriving in a country, their expenditure, and their average length of stay.
  • Purpose of Tourism: Distinguish between leisure tourism and business tourism, examining the proportion of each for different countries and its evolution over time.
Data Analysis Techniques:

We applied various statistical models and clustering algorithms to identify trends and segment countries. Techniques like time-series analysis, k-means clustering, and data visualization were used to extract insights from tourism-related data.

Results:

The project identified significant groupings among countries that helped us understand how tourism changes in response to economic, social, and political events. The analysis also provided insights into how different variables, such as transportation methods and tourist spending, evolved over time and how these factors relate to larger global trends.
